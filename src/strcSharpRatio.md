## **Quantitative Validation of the 5.37 Sharpe Ratio**
The assertion by Michael Saylor and Phong Le that \$STRC achieved a Sharpe Ratio of 5.37 in March 2026 requires a granular decomposition of the underlying statistical inputs. The Sharpe Ratio is defined as the excess return of an asset over the risk-free rate, divided by the standard deviation of those excess returns. For \$STRC, the calculation is predicated on an annualized yield that has consistently outperformed traditional high-yield benchmarks, paired with a 30-day volatility profile that reached historic lows in the first quarter of 2026\ [1]

The reported 5.37 figure is an evolution of earlier performance data. On March 11, 2026, the Sharpe Ratio for \$STRC was noted at 3.08, which already surpassed major technology stocks and gold [2] The subsequent jump to 5.37 by March 18 was driven primarily by a collapse in realized volatility. While the instrument launched in July 2025 with an annualized volatility of approximately 14%, its 30-day historical volatility dropped to a record low of 1.5% in mid-March 2026\ [1] This 1.5% volatility level is remarkably low for a security linked to an underlying asset as volatile as Bitcoin, which often maintains annualized volatility in excess of 50% [4]

### **Mathematical Decomposition of the Performance Metric**
To validate the 5.37 ratio, the standard Sharpe Ratio formula is applied:

$$S = \frac{R_p - R_f}{\sigma_p}$$  

In this context, $R_p$ represents the annualized total return, which is largely comprised of the 11.53% forward dividend yield [3] $R_f$ is the risk-free rate, and $\sigma_p$ is the annualized standard deviation. When the denominator ($\sigma_p$) is reduced to a 30-day annualized level of 1.5%, the resulting quotient expands dramatically. This validates the 5.37 figure as a mathematically accurate representation of the instrument's risk-adjusted efficiency during the specific window of observation in March 2026\ [1]

The stability of this ratio is maintained by the variable dividend mechanism. MicroStrategy adjusts the monthly payout specifically to absorb market fluctuations and keep the share price anchored to its 100 USD par value [5] By increasing the dividend when the market price dips, the company creates a synthetic floor for the stock, effectively transferring volatility from the price of the security into the variable yield paid to the investor [5]

### **Leverage and the "BTC Yield" KPI**
