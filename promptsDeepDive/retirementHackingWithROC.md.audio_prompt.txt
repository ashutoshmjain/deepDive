Next, we'll walk through the model presented in the paper. It follows a couple, aged 57, who start investing USD 10,400 annually for 10 years into a perpetual preferred stock. We'll show you the math behind how this specific, disciplined approach compounds over time.

The magic happens at age 67. We'll explain how they begin to sell shares to create that USD 20,000 income. Because of the ROC distributions, their cost basis on the earliest shares is extremely low, but not zero. The episode will detail how the sale generates a taxable gain of only about USD 18,000, which falls entirely within the standard deduction for a married couple over 65, resulting in zero federal taxes.

Crucially, we'll discuss the concept of the "tax cushion." This strategy is so efficient that it leaves over USD 45,000 of their tax-free capital gains allowance untouched. This means they can sell other assets, like from a 401(k) or another brokerage account, and still pay no federal tax.
