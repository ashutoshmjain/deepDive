So let's break down this battle for the trillions. The game-changer was Executive Order 14330, signed in August 2025. It ordered the Department of Labor to stand down from its 2022 threats and, more importantly, to create a "Safe Harbor" for employers. This means your boss might finally be able to offer a Bitcoin ETF without the fear of being sued into oblivion.

This is the difference between the "Window" and the "Menu." Right now, you might be able to buy a Bitcoin ETF through a clunky, high-friction "Self-Directed Brokerage Window." But less than 3% of people use that. The real prize is the "Core Menu"—the default list of 10-20 funds your 401(k) offers.

And the math is staggering. If just 1% of the USD 9.3 trillion in 401(k)s flows into Bitcoin ETFs, that's a USD 93 billion buy order. That's almost half of all the liquid Bitcoin available on exchanges today. If that allocation goes to 3% or 5%, you're talking about hundreds of billions in new capital. This isn't about active traders; it's about passive, automatic flows from millions of Americans' paychecks, happening every two weeks, forever.
