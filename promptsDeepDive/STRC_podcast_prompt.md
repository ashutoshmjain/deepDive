It offers a high dividend of around 10%, but the real kicker is its tax treatment. The dividends are classified as a "Return of Capital" or ROC.

Explain what ROC means in simple terms: instead of paying tax on the dividend income now, it reduces your cost basis in the stock, deferring the tax until you sell. This makes it incredibly attractive for a few key groups. We're talking about Bogleheads who love high-yield, stable investments, but also high-earners who are looking for ways to defer their tax burden.
