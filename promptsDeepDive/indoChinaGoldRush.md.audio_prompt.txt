**Main Episode with drum rolls:**

Let's dig into this "Golden Pivot." Central banks (China, India) are strategically rebalancing reserves, reducing dollar reliance, and hedging against asset freezes. They're repatriating gold, asserting sovereignty, and shielding from US Treasury volatility.

This creates a profound "wealth effect." India's trillions in household gold—once "dead capital"—now fuels a massive surge in gold-backed credit. Small businesses and households access low-interest loans, formalizing operations and driving growth.

The Asian gold market has fascinating sociocultural inelasticity. Unlike Western markets, selling gold carries deep cultural stigma. Even parabolic price increases won't trigger a supply flood, intensifying central bank competition and driving gold's ascent past $5,000.
